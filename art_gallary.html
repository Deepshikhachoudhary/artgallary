Famous Portraits Art Gallery

Welcome to a curated collection of iconic portrait artworks from renowned artists.

Mona Lisa

Mona Lisa |

Artist: Leonardo da Vinci
Medium: Oil on poplar panel
The Mona Lisa, painted between 1503 and 1519, is one of the most famous portraits in history.
Her enigmatic smile and soft gaze have captivated audiences for centuries.
Originally believed to be unfinished, it is now considered a complete masterpiece.

M. F. Husain – "Mother Teresa"

M. F. Husain

Artist: M. F. Husain
Medium: Oil on canvas
This touching portrait of Mother Teresa highlights her compassion and humanity.
Husain uses expressive lines and gentle tones to honor her service.
This is one of his most respectful works.

Raja Ravi Varma – "Lady in the Moonlight"

Raja Ravi Varma |

Artist: Raja Ravi Varma
Medium: Oil on canvas
This painting reflects the beauty of traditional Indian womanhood under a moonlit sky.
Ravi Varma masterfully combined Indian subjects with European realism.

S. H. Raza – "Bindu"

S. H. Raza

Artist: S. H. Raza
Medium: Acrylic on canvas
The Bindu represents the seed of creation and spiritual energy.
This abstract yet symbolic work uses bold colors and geometric shapes.
Early works of Raza were more figurative.

Frida Kahlo – "Self-Portrait with Thorn Necklace"

Frida Kahlo

Artist: Frida Kahlo
Medium: Oil on canvas
In this self-portrait, Kahlo explores pain, identity, and resilience.
The thorn necklace and calm gaze make this one of her most personal works.
It also features symbolism through animals and plants.
